GENESIS 001 · COVENANT OF THE HEGEMONY

The Imperial Charter of Admission

The gates do not open for the many. They open for the sworn.

Social Contract version 2026-07-19-HEGEMONY-SOCIAL-CONTRACT-V3 · Entry-agreement bundle 2026-07-19-hegemony-entry-agreement-v3 · Last updated 19 July 2026

The Hegemony is game fiction. Nothing in this Charter creates real-world political, religious, military, or personal allegiance, obligation, or duty. All references to empire, sacrifice, citizenship, and loyalty describe in-game systems and roleplay only. This is a conduct covenant for the official experimental Alpha.

Article I · The Realm endures beyond any citizen

Admission to the Hegemony is a grant of the Empire, not a possession of the citizen. It confers no ownership of a server slot, no property right, and no promise of permanent access. Citizens keep the Realm whole: no cheating, no sabotage of authoritative state, no impersonation of another Keeper, no coordinated abusive multi-account activity, no exploitation of vulnerabilities for advantage. Those who discover a flaw in the Realm's walls report it privately under the Security Policy — quietly, as befits a loyal citizen.

Article II · The Empire claims your legions, not your life

The Hegemony wages eternal campaign, and campaigns are fed with soldiers. In-game troops, units, and assets raised under Hegemony banners exist to be spent — committed, expended, and sacrificed for imperial objectives as game systems define. Losses in service of the Empire are the intended price of citizenship, not a defect. This Article governs only fictional in-game units within implemented systems. No sacrifice mechanic exists until its rules and server authority are implemented and published, and no such mechanic will ever require real-world payment, action, or harm.

Article III · Tribute is final

Where implemented and published, citizens may burn eligible external tokens as tribute to receive in-game premium accounting units. Tribute is voluntary, irreversible, and non-refundable. Premium units are experimental game-accounting entries with no cash value; they are non-transferable, non-redeemable, and confer no ownership, investment, equity, security, or expectation of profit or reward. Citizenship remains revocable under Article V regardless of any tribute rendered; render tribute only if you accept this. No burn creates entitlement to future features, access, or restitution.

Article IV · Citizens behind the keeps are not game pieces

War belongs inside the Realm. Rivalry, conquest, deception in play, and merciless in-game strategy are the lifeblood of the Hegemony. But the human being behind every keep stands outside the fiction: threats, doxxing, stalking, fraud, account compromise, malware, serious targeted harassment, abusive automation, and attempts to reach another person through non-public data are crimes against the Realm itself. Follow applicable law and respect reasonable boundaries outside the game.

Article V · The standard of citizenship

The Hegemony is small by design and admitted by invitation alone. With rarity comes standard: citizens are held to exemplary conduct in and around the Realm — honesty in play, integrity toward other participants, and adherence to this Charter, the Alpha Terms, and applicable law. Internal misconduct by a citizen may result in immediate revocation of admission without prior warning, because the trust extended at the gate is the trust that may be withdrawn. Where safety and law permit, a former citizen may request one reconsideration through the published contact route. This standard governs observable conduct only; it does not police beliefs, opinions, identities, or lawful life outside the game.

Article VI · Honest counsel is loyalty, not treason

The throne is served best by those who speak plainly. Criticizing Warpkeep, its fiction, its maintainer, its rules, or a proposed feature is not misconduct and never grounds for revocation. Citizens may question decisions, report defects, and dissent without praising the Hegemony. Feedback and open-source contributions may shape the world, but no suggestion or contribution is guaranteed adoption or in-game representation. Flattery buys nothing; candor costs nothing.

Article VII · The Ousters beyond the reach

Beyond the Empire's borders, the Ousters will one day gather — open to all, sworn to none. When implemented and published, Ouster participation will be free to join, will carry no citizenship standard, and will be subject only to applicable law and the Terms of Service; violation of those alone may result in enforcement up to and including IP-level bans. Ousters hold no premium tribute path and no imperial protection. Until Ouster systems are implemented and published with server authority, this Article creates no rules, rights, factions, or entitlements — it is a horizon, not a border.

Article VIII · A world still being forged

The current Alpha has no complete core strategy loop, alliance system, combat system, sacrifice system, or in-game chat. Lore, this Charter's fiction, and design drafts create no hidden rules, paid power, obligations, punishments, rewards, or future entitlements. A system governs participants only after its rules and server authority are implemented and published.

Article IX · Judgment of the Realm

Warpkeep may warn, limit, suspend, or revoke official Alpha access when observable conduct violates this Charter, the Alpha Terms, law, or the safety and integrity of the service. Immediate containment — including the immediate revocation described in Article V — may be used where delay creates material risk or where citizen misconduct breaches the trust of admission. Durable decisions should consider context, severity, repetition, intent, impact, and available evidence.

Article X · Seal and succession

This exact Charter is incorporated into entry-agreement bundle 2026-07-19-hegemony-entry-agreement-v3. Material changes require a later visible version and a new unchecked acceptance; earlier evidence never authorizes a later bundle. Public questions may be raised through the Warpkeep repository without posting private account, authentication, wallet, or moderation evidence. Security concerns belong in the repository's Security Policy.
