Hegemony Social Contract

The gates open by trust, and the Realm is shared.

Social Contract version 2026-07-19-hegemony-social-contract-v2 · Entry-agreement bundle 2026-07-19-hegemony-entry-agreement-v2 · Last updated 19 July 2026

This is a conduct covenant for the official experimental Alpha. Hegemony is game fiction, not real-world political, religious, or personal allegiance.

1. Care for the shared Realm

Admission is an invitation, not ownership of a server slot or a promise of permanent access. Play honestly. Do not cheat, sabotage authoritative state, impersonate another Keeper, coordinate abusive multi-account activity, or use a vulnerability for advantage. Report security flaws privately under the Security Policy.

2. People are not game pieces

Rivalry and criticism are welcome; threats, doxxing, stalking, fraud, account compromise, malware, serious targeted harassment, abusive automation, and attempts to reach another person through non-public data are not. Follow applicable law and respect reasonable boundaries outside the game.

3. Good-faith dissent belongs here

Criticizing Warpkeep, its fiction, its maintainer, its rules, or a proposed feature is not misconduct. Players may question decisions, report defects, and disagree without praising the Hegemony. Community feedback and open-source contributions may shape the world, but no suggestion or contribution is guaranteed to be adopted or represented in-game.

4. An unfinished world

The current Alpha has no complete core strategy loop, alliance system, combat system, or in-game chat. Lore and design drafts do not create hidden rules, paid power, obligations, punishments, rewards, or future entitlements. A system governs players only after its rules and server authority are implemented and published.

5. Proportionate protection

Warpkeep may warn, limit, suspend, or revoke official Alpha access when observable conduct violates this covenant, the Alpha Terms, law, or the safety and integrity of the service. Immediate containment may be used where delay creates material risk. Durable decisions should consider context, severity, repetition, intent, impact, and available evidence. Where safety and law permit, a participant may request one reconsideration through the published contact route.

6. Version and contact

This exact Social Contract is incorporated into entry-agreement bundle 2026-07-19-hegemony-entry-agreement-v2. Material changes require a later visible version and a new unchecked acceptance; earlier evidence never authorizes a later bundle. Public questions may be raised through the Warpkeep repository without posting private account, authentication, wallet, or moderation evidence. Security concerns belong in the repository's Security Policy.
