The Hegemony Empire · Covenant of admission

Hegemony Social Contract

The gates of Genesis 001 open by trust, not by right.

Version 1 · Social Contract version 2026-07-18-hegemony-social-contract-v1 · Entry-agreement bundle 2026-07-18-hegemony-entry-agreement-v1 · Last updated 18 July 2026

This is a project-authored social covenant for the experimental Warpkeep Alpha. It governs conduct and membership in the official Hegemony realm; it is not a declaration of real-world political, religious, or personal allegiance. Formal legal review remains necessary before public factions, chat, paid power, or large-scale operation.

Current Alpha: Only the Hegemony Empire is playable. Warpkeep remains allowlist and admission gated. The Ousters and the Core are provisional future setting concepts; neither is playable or in active development, and neither is promised to ship in its present form.

1. The three powers

Three powers cast their shadows across the unwritten marches. The Hegemony Empire keeps the first walls, binds scattered keeps to a common banner, and asks discipline in exchange for belonging. Beyond its law stand the Ousters, a provisional future idea of freeholds, bargain, rivalry, migration, and chosen allegiance. Deeper still waits the Core, a provisional future image of sealed vaults, old engines, and hidden designs whose voice has not been written.

Today these are not three playable factions. The current Alpha contains only the Hegemony founding cohort. The Ousters and the Core establish narrative direction. They make no access or feature promise, release date, entitlement, access path, or present game rule. They are nonplayable and not in active development; their names remain provisional pending separate naming and originality review.

2. The gate is a trust

Admission to the Hegemony Empire is an invitation into the official Warpkeep realm. It is not purchased property, permanent tenure, ownership of a server slot, or a promise of access forever. A Keeper enters by the project’s approval and remains while their observable conduct is compatible with this covenant, the Alpha Terms, the safety of other participants, and the integrity of the realm.

The Hegemony does not inquire into a player’s lawful private beliefs or demand agreement with the maintainer outside Warpkeep. This is not a real-world political program. The project judges conduct in, or directly affecting, the official service; it does not claim authority over a person’s life beyond it.

3. The Imperial charge

The Hegemony fiction values a shared cause over private advantage. Future Imperial play may someday ask a keep to commit time, resources, scouts, or fortified ground to a published common defence. Any such game choice could carry strategic loss inside the game, but no future story or rule permits real-world coercion, surrender of legal rights, transfer of external property, access to private accounts, or action outside the service.

No levy, command system, army sacrifice, faction war, or collective obligation is playable today. If any becomes playable, its authority, limits, costs, refusal consequences, recovery rules, and safety controls must be implemented and published before it governs player state.

4. Order within the ranks

The Empire can survive defeat; it cannot safely operate through deliberate corruption of trust. Do not knowingly sabotage official operations, cheat, exploit a vulnerability for advantage, impersonate another Keeper, coordinate hostile multi-account activity, disclose genuinely non-public game information contrary to published rules, fraudulently represent an official order, threaten participants, expose private information, or use the service to pursue another person beyond reasonable game conflict.

Good-faith criticism is not disorder. Strategic debate, criticism of Warpkeep or its fiction, criticism of the maintainer, refusal to praise the Hegemony, reporting defects, questioning monetisation, and arguing that a proposed order is unwise are not violations merely because they are uncomfortable. Dissent means disagreement about ideas or choices; misconduct means observable conduct that materially undermines safety, fair operation, or another person’s ability to participate.

5. The common law of the realm

Some rules stand above banners. Every present or future participant remains subject to the Alpha Terms, applicable law, service-security rules, anti-cheat rules, and the universal protection of participants from serious abuse. No faction culture creates an exception for credible threats, doxxing, stalking, malicious exploitation, malware, fraud, account compromise, severe targeted harassment, abusive automation, access-control bypass, or unlawful content.

Faction rivalry may someday be fierce. Human beings are not battlefield objects. Imperial membership discipline is the limited conduct covenant above; this common safety floor protects everyone regardless of banner, viewpoint, or future role.

6. The Ousters — a future beyond Imperial law

The Ousters are conceived as a future open culture beyond the Empire’s admission covenant. Their fiction may value free bargain, shifting allegiance, internal trade, private war, and self-rule over Imperial discipline. That possibility does not make the Ousters immoral, unlawful, disposable, or a punishment faction; universal safety and service rules would still apply to every participant.

None of this is playable today. Ousters are not open for registration, cannot receive an expelled Hegemony keep, cannot trade, cannot attack, cannot cause neutralisation, and have no current faction state. Any future conversion, internal conflict, or recovery path would need separately published, authoritative, abuse-resistant rules before it affects player state.

7. The Core — the veiled third power

The Core is reserved as a future in-world power of old engines and hidden law. A later story might use clearly identified machine intelligence systems or appointed game stewards, but its purpose, voice, and relation to the two human cultures remain unwritten. That future possibility gives no system hidden authority today.

The Core is not currently playable, active, autonomous, or in development as a faction system. It does not moderate players, decide admission, issue sanctions, command armies, alter the world, or exercise authority over player accounts. Any future automated or steward-operated actor must have published limits, server-owned authority, observable safeguards, and a separation from legal moderation decisions unless a later reviewed policy lawfully says otherwise.

8. Unequal banners, published rules

Warpkeep does not promise equal factions or symmetrical power. Future cultures may differ in admission, trade, internal conflict, military obligation, coordination, recovery, and access to faction-specific systems. Asymmetry is not permission for hidden rules: a system governs players only after its authority, costs, risks, and limits are implemented and published.

No faction-specific purchase, token burn, premium power, treasury, trade privilege, combat protection, castle conversion, or punishment exists merely because it appears in lore or design discussion. Community Marks retain their current published status; this covenant does not make Marks spendable, transferable, redeemable, purchasable, or convertible into power.

9. Judgment at the gate

Warpkeep may warn, limit official-service access, suspend access, revoke Hegemony admission, or remove access to the official service when observable conduct violates the Alpha Terms, this covenant, applicable law, or the safety and integrity of the realm. Immediate protective action may be taken where delay could expose a person, private data, the service, or authoritative state to harm.

Durable decisions should use context-sensitive human judgment informed by severity, repetition, intent, impact, evidence, and risk. Technical automation may contain obvious abuse, but it does not make an unreviewable declaration of a person’s moral character or legal guilt. Where safety, security, or law permits, the project should identify the rule and general reason for a restriction. A player may request one reconsideration through the published contact route; a restriction may remain while that review occurs.

During the current Hegemony-only Alpha, revocation of Hegemony admission also removes current playable access because no other faction is playable. It does not convert a keep, grant a neutral status, or decide a person’s standing outside Warpkeep.

10. Speech and future chat

No in-game chat is presently playable. This covenant does not create a chat channel, message-retention system, reporting queue, AI moderation, or a moderation database. If shared chat is ever considered, the project must first publish who can read it, what is retained, how abuse can be reported, what automated tools are used, and the applicable privacy and retention rules.

11. Changes to the covenant

The version and date above identify this covenant. This exact version is incorporated into the Alpha Terms as entry-agreement bundle 2026-07-18-hegemony-entry-agreement-v1. One checkbox accepts the current Alpha Terms and this Social Contract, not the Privacy Notice as blanket privacy consent. After authenticated acknowledgement, the implementation records only private immutable evidence of the verified FID, exact bundle version, and acceptance time.

Material changes require a later visible entry-agreement version and a new unchecked intentional acceptance before current entry or gameplay continues. Earlier immutable evidence remains an audit record and can preserve a previously public Community Marks projection; it never authorizes the later bundle. Questions may be raised through the public Warpkeep GitHub repository without posting private account, authentication, wallet, or moderation evidence. For a security concern, use the repository’s Security Policy.
